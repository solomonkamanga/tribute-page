Here's a timeline of Professor Quaynor's life.

  • 1949

    Born in Accra, Ghana, West Africa. And he attended Adisadel College and Achimota School.

  • 1973

    Prof. Quaynor graduated in Engineering Science from Dartmouth College in 1972 and received a Bachelor of Engineering degree from the Thayer School of Engineering there in 1973.

  • 1974

    Studied Computer Science and obtained an Master of Science degree from the State University of New York at StonyBrook.

  • 1977

    Obtained an Ph.D. from the State University of New York at StonyBrook.

  • 1994

    Prof. Quaynor established the first internet service provider in Ghana and West Africa, operated by Network Computer Systems Ltd. He then assisted in implementing the transformational new technology throughout sub-Saharan Africa.

  • 2000

    Dr. Quaynor was elected as the At-Large Director of the African region to the ICANN Board. And served until June, 2003.

  • 2001

    He was the founding chairman of AFRINIC, he regional Internet registry (RIR) for Africa.

  • 2007

    In December 2007, Quaynor was awarded the Jonathan B. Postel Service Award from the IETF (The Internet Engineering Task Force).

  • 2013

    On 26 June 2013, he was inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame by the Internet Society.

  • 2015

    He was awarded the 2015 ICANN Multi-stakeholder Ethos Award together with Cheryl Langdon Orr.

  • “Africa will miss a great development opportunity in much the same way Africa lost on the industrial revolution, unless serious and truly committed efforts are made by Africa to address the rapid expansion on the internet-user gap between Africa and industrialised countries”

    Professor Nii Narku Quaynor, circa 2014
